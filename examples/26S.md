# Case Study: The 26S Proteasome

> **Status: Capability Demonstration. Results Invalid.**
> This study is published as a transparent record of an LLM-driven genomic research workflow — what it produced, where it failed, and what the process reveals about working with LLMs in a research context.

---

## The Research Question

> *Treat the 26S Proteasome as a mechanically redundant 3D machine and map every missense variant from the KGP individuals across all 33 subunits. Perform a spatial analysis to determine if pathogenic variation is statistically partitioned toward the distal 'Lid' (Zone C) rather than the more evolutionary constrained 'Core' (Zone A) or 'Gating' (Zone B) interfaces. Identify individuals with a high cumulative burden (2+ 'Likely Pathogenic' variants) to investigate inter-subunit compensation, searching for paired 'weakening' and 'stabilizing' mutations at protein-protein hinges. Finally, define the 'mechanical tolerance' of the proteasome by establishing the maximum cumulative structural disruption observed in a single healthy individual based on AlphaMissense scores and calculated ΔΔG values.*

The question requires data retrieval across 33 genes, structural zone classification, per-individual burden computation, 3D distance mapping onto a cryo-EM structure, statistical correlation analysis, and synthesis into a coherent narrative. It was posed to two AI models working in concert.

---

## The Workflow: Three Roles

**The PI Model** (_Google Gemini_) was responsible for scientific direction. It formulated the initial hypothesis, decomposed it into discrete executable tasks, reviewed each task report as it was completed, and made decisions about where to focus next. When early data failed to support the initial hypothesis, the PI model revised its direction, generated new tasks, and iterated. It was rare for the initial hypothesis to survive contact with the data intact.

**The Research Model** (_Claude Sonnet_) did the scientific work. For every task issued by the PI, it queried the KGP dataset via the OneKGPd MCP server, processed the data, wrote and executed analysis scripts, ran statistical tests, interpreted results, and produced structured written reports.

**The Validation Role** was responsible for quality control, process facilitation, and final reviews. It was shared between humans and models. Independent verification, free from the assumptions of the PI and Research models, played an essential part.

## Why the Results Are Invalid

The PI model provided genomic coordinates for all 33 proteasome subunit genes as part of the initial task specification. The Research model was instructed to treat these coordinates as ground truth.

One gene's coordinates were wrong. That error propagated through every downstream task — variant counts, zone classification, burden computation, spatial mapping, statistical tests. When the correct coordinates were applied and the analysis rerun, the signal disappeared. The statistical tests were no longer significant.

This is not a failure of the Research model. It is a demonstration of a real constraint in multi-model pipelines: garbage in, garbage out.

The error was caught by a validation process designed to be free of the original assumptions. Validation scripts retrieved the raw data directly from the variant store and cross-validated results. Claude generated scripts in new sessions based on high-level spec only, entirely bypassing the MCP Server and the assumptions of the PI and Research models.

## The Value of Models

The analytical work spanned 33 genes, burden computation across 3,202 individuals, 3D spatial mapping, and multiple statistical frameworks — all executed by LLMs without manual intervention. That alone was of substantial value, but it wasn't the most important contribution.

Throughout the study, the PI model repeatedly revised the research direction as task reports returned data inconsistent with prior assumptions. When results contradicted the working hypothesis, it generated new hypotheses grounded in published literature and decomposed them into the next round of tasks. The task sequence in this study reflects that iterative process: the final set of tasks looked substantially different from the initial plan.

The manuscript drafting phase demonstrated the same capability, under more extreme conditions.

**The coordinate error.** The genomic coordinates assigned to PSMB6 by the PI model pointed to a region approximately 175kb downstream of the gene, overlapping SLC13A5 (Solute Carrier Family 13 Member 5) and a PSMB6-SLC13A5 read-through transcript. As a result, the task reports throughout this study contain variants attributed to PSMB6 that are in reality in a different gene. The statistics, burden calculations, and spatial analyses reflect that misattribution.

**The β6/β1 error.** The task reports consistently describe PSMB6 as encoding the β6 subunit — a non-catalytic structural component — and use this to explain the apparent tolerance for LP variants in what was believed to be the 20S catalytic core. This characterization is also incorrect: PSMB6 encodes β1, one of three catalytic subunits carrying the threonine nucleophile responsible for proteolysis. This error was identified during manuscript drafting.

Both initial drafts were built on the β6/non-catalytic assumption. When the β1 correction arrived, the non-catalytic explanation was no longer available. The Sonnet manuscript needed a different biological mechanism to explain why variants of such high predicted pathogenicity in a catalytic subunit are tolerated in the dataset.

The explanation Sonnet found was the immunoproteasome: three interferon-γ-inducible genes (PSMB9/LMP2, PSMB8/LMP7, PSMB10/MECL-1) encode alternative catalytic β-subunits that replace their constitutive counterparts during immunoproteasome assembly. PSMB9 is a direct replacement for PSMB6/β1. An individual with impaired constitutive β1 retains a functional dedicated backup on a different chromosome. This mechanism represents a genuine architectural feature of the human immune system that structure-based pathogenicity predictors do not model.

The explanation is scientifically sound. It was produced in response to a situation created entirely by data errors — the variants were not in PSMB6, and the homozygous tolerance was not a proteasome phenomenon at all. But the biological reasoning that identified the immunoproteasome compensation mechanism is independent of whether the underlying data was valid. It reflects command of structural biology and immunology literature, applied to an apparently impossible situation.

The Opus draft, which was not updated after the β1 correction, retains the non-catalytic β6 explanation — preserved exactly as the task reports left it.

## Dataset Limitations

Even with valid inputs, the KGP dataset imposes hard limits on what conclusions are achievable. Variants with subtle, late-onset, or population-specific effects are indistinguishable from benign variation without phenotypic data. Statistical power for rare variant analysis is limited at n=3,202. Ancestry representation is unequal. For clinical and research cohorts with phenotypic data and larger or more targeted sample sizes, these limitations are substantially reduced.
